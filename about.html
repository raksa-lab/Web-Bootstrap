A B O U T

The Union of European Football Associations (UEFA), established on 15 June 1954, is the governing body for football in Europe and one of six continental confederations under FIFA. Based in Nyon, Switzerland, UEFA oversees European football, futsal, and beach soccer at both club and national levels. With the motto "We care about football," it unites 55 national football associations across Europe. Aleksander Čeferin has served as UEFA's president since 2016.

Cristiano Ronaldo

Cristiano Ronaldo, born on February 5, 1985, in Portugal, is one of football’s all-time greats. He has won 5 Ballon d'Or awards and played for top clubs like Manchester United, Real Madrid, and Juventus. Ronaldo is also Portugal’s all-time leading scorer.

Lionel Messi

Lionel Messi, born on June 24, 1987, in Argentina, is one of football’s greatest players. He has won 7 Ballon d'Or awards, 4 Champions League titles, and the 2022 FIFA World Cup. Messi has played for Barcelona, Paris Saint-Germain, and Inter Miami.

Carlo Ancelotti

Carlo Ancelotti is an Italian football manager with success at top clubs like AC Milan, Chelsea, and Real Madrid. He has won several Champions League titles and is regarded as one of the best in the game.

Zidane

Zinedine Zidane is a French football legend and former manager, known for his skillful play and leadership. He won the FIFA World Cup in 1998 and UEFA Champions League titles as both a player and manager with Real Madrid.

Pep Guardiola

Pep Guardiola is a Spanish football manager known for his tactical expertise and possession-based style. He has won numerous Premier League and Champions League titles with Manchester City and Barcelona.
